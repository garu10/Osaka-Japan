Rich Culture

Osaka’s culture reflects a unique blend of humor, warmth, and tradition. From its famous performing arts to centuries-old festivals and street fashion, the city embodies the lively and generous spirit of its people — the Osakans, known for their friendliness and wit.

“Kuidaore” Spirit

The word “Kuidaore” (食い倒れ) means “to eat oneself into ruin,” symbolizing Osaka’s deep love for food and celebration. It’s more than dining — it’s about enjoying life’s pleasures with laughter, flavor, and good company.

Traditional Arts & Performances

Osaka has a deep connection to Japan’s classic performing arts, including Bunraku and Kabuki.

Bunraku Puppet Theater

Originating in Osaka during the Edo period, Bunraku is a traditional puppet theatre accompanied by shamisen music and narrative chanting.

Kabuki Performances

Dotonbori’s Shochikuza Theatre hosts Kabuki — known for its dramatic acting, colorful costumes, and live orchestra.

Manzai Comedy

Manzai — Osaka’s rapid-fire style of stand-up comedy — features duos exchanging clever jokes and playful banter.

Colorful Festivals

Experience the city’s joy and unity through its centuries-old festivals.

One of Japan’s top three festivals, Tenjin Matsuri celebrates the deity of scholarship with river processions, fireworks, and music — held every July along the Okawa River.

This thrilling September festival features massive wooden floats pulled at high speeds through the streets. It’s a symbol of strength, teamwork, and Osaka’s fearless energy.

Celebrated in August, the Sumiyoshi Festival marks the end of Osaka’s summer with elegant Shinto processions and traditional dance performances.
